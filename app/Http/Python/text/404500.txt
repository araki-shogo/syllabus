This course is mainly concerned with some of the major issues in the world today, including the i
ssues of education and gender, global warming, terrorism, poverty and hunger, refugee, as well 
as weapons and war. A central theme concerns the struggle for peace in a world that has experi
enced a great deal of human suffering and conflict. Topics have both historical and contemporar
y implications for students to rethink the international relations history and better understand t
he contemporary global world.�

This course aims to provide students with basic knowledge and perspectives about global studie
s, in an effort to cultivate the spirit of internationalism and global mind. The class is completely 
conducted in English based on the textbook. It attempts to help students to gain the ability and 
skills to understand historical and contemporary global issues in English, as well as think and disc
uss about these issues in English. It is also expected that students would further develop their in
terest reading about global issues through English literature and materials on their own in order 
to broaden their global perspectives.�
